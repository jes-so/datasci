# Childcare Cost Burden in the United States

This project explores the childcare cost burden in the United States. It consists of two main arms: Informational advocacy for social workers and adjacent fields,
and direct advocacy to legislators and adjacent legal roles.

## Outcomes

On average, childcare cost burden exceeds the affordability benchmark for childcare set by the federal Child Care and Development Fund (CCDF) Program at 7%
of household income. Infant childcare exceeds 15% of household income on average, toddler childcare exceeds 13%, and school age childcare exceeds 11%.
Childcare payments are comparable to grocery or even mortgage payment monthly costs, with a majority of households paying between 15-20% of their
monthly household income on childcare and some households exceeding 50%.
