# Predicting Homelessness Rates From Housing and Urban Development Data

This project predicts homelessness rates from data provided by HUD (Housing and Urban Development) using random forest modeling
techniques. It leverages many relevant predictors, such as income and SSD participation in an area, to predict homelessness rates
and suggest remediation programs to improve outcomes.

## Outcomes

The applied model can predict homelessness within 11% of actual rates. This model is based on the most important predictors, which
were identified as the availability of subsidized housing; the SSI participation rate (overall regional poverty); the urbanicity of
an area; housing market density; and the difference over 5 years in severe housing cost burden.
